Design System

The System

The visible parts of credibility: an editorial type voice, a warm paper foundation, border-first depth, and exactly one saturated color reserved for conversion. Every token below is shared across all six pages.

Typography

Fraunces display, Inter text

A high-contrast editorial serif for headings paired with Inter for body and UI. Headings run tight; body relaxes to 1.6 for a comfortable reading measure. Georgia is retired.

H1 · Display Fraunces · clamp 2.5–4rem · LH 1.05

Drink smarter, not harder

H2 · Display Fraunces · clamp 1.75–2.5rem · LH 1.15

The science of next-day clarity

H3 · Display Fraunces · 1.5rem · LH 1.15

How DHM supports the liver

Body · Text Inter · 1.0625rem (17px) · LH 1.6

Dihydromyricetin (DHM) is a flavonoid studied for its role in supporting the body’s natural alcohol metabolism. Seventeen-pixel body text and a relaxed line height keep long-form research readable.

Small · Text Inter · 0.875rem · LH 1.5

Captions, metadata, disclosures, and footnotes — quieter ink, same Inter rhythm.

Color

Warm paper, green demoted, orange protected

Green keeps its brand equity but stops shouting. Calm neutrals on warm paper let the single orange conversion accent become the natural focal point. Blue is reserved for links and science callouts only.

Elevation

Border-first depth, not shadow-on-everything

Each surface earns at most one depth cue. Most content sits flat on paper with a single warm border — the biggest modern tell. Shadows are reserved for things that genuinely lift off the page.

Tier 0

Flat

Border-first. 1px warm hairline, --elev-0, no shadow. The default for most content.

Tier 1

Raised

Interactive cards. Two-layer warm soft shadow (--elev-1) plus border. Hover to lift.

Tier 2

Overlay

Modals, dropdowns, sticky bars. A real shadow (--elev-2) for things that float.

Shape & Rhythm

One radius, a strict 4 / 8 grid

An 8px radius unifies cards, buttons, and inputs; pills handle chips and badges. Spacing snaps to a 4/8 scale so vertical rhythm never drifts.

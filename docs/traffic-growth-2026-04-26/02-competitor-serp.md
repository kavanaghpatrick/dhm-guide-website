## 2. Per-Competitor Content Audit

### 2.1 Healthline — *the search-share leader for hangover queries*
- **Hub URL:** healthline.com/nutrition/7-ways-to-prevent-a-hangover
- **Word count:** ~1,800 (modest! — wins on authority, not length)
- **Heading pattern:** What is X / Before / While / How to cure / Takeaway (sequential, scannable)
- **Trust signals:** Named author with credentials (Atli Arnarson BSc, PhD) + medical reviewer (Jared Meacham, PhD, RD, CSCS), photo bylines, last-updated date, [1-7] inline citation numbering
- **Schema:** FAQ + HowTo (numbered tips); fully cited to NIAAA and NIH
- **Win factor:** **E-E-A-T at structural level** — every hangover article has medical reviewer

### 2.2 Cleveland Clinic — *medical-institution authority*
- **Hub URL:** my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/16627-hangover
- **Word count:** ~1,800-2,000
- **H1/H2 pattern:** Single H1 "Hangover" + H2 sections [What Is / Symptoms and Causes / Management / Prevention] — *health-disease taxonomy structure*
- **Trust signals:** "Medically Reviewed. Last updated 04/06/2026" badge; institutional non-profit footer; multi-location mentions (Abu Dhabi, Florida, Ohio); 7 peer-reviewed citations
- **Win factor:** Domain-level authority + freshness badge ("Last updated 04/06/2026")

### 2.3 NIH/PMC — *citation source for the entire niche*
- The PMC3292407 paper (Shen et al., 2012, "DHM as a Novel Anti-Alcohol Intoxication Medication") is the **single most-cited DHM paper on the open web**.
- The NIH LiverTox monograph (NBK594407) is the **default authority page for DHM safety**.
- **Reproducible insight:** Every brand site (Flyby, Cheers, ZBiotics, Sunset) cites these same 2-3 sources. DHM Guide should *summarize and visualize* these papers in dedicated study-summary posts that earn the citation links going to PMC right now.

### 2.4 USC Today — *USC press release on Silva 2020 liver-protection study*
- Single page, but consistently ranks because it sits on a high-authority domain (.edu) and contains the press-friendly quote "DHM activates a cascade of mechanisms that erase alcohol from the body very quickly."
- **Win factor:** Authority (.edu) + extraction-ready quotes that get cited everywhere.

### 2.5 Better Results Book / Jacked Gorilla — *affiliate listicles*
- ~3,500-4,500 words; product-comparison structure with **standardized cards (pros/cons, dose, price)**
- Hooks: comparison tables with $/dose, "Top 3 Picks" summary, dosage guidelines correlated to drinking levels
- Author bios are thin (Better Results Book has none beyond name; Jacked Gorilla has "Matt McIntyre, two decades lifting")
- **Why they outrank DHM Guide on commercial queries:** comprehensive product breadth + standardized comparison structure, not authority

### 2.6 ZBiotics — *brand-as-publisher exemplar*
- "Lab Notes" hub at /blogs/journal
- Dedicated explainer pages: "How Pre-Alcohol Works," "How Sugar-to-Fiber Works," "How Your Body Breaks Down Alcohol," "How We Engineer Probiotics"
- Topic-cluster architecture ties educational content to product pages
- Cites PubMed/NIH inline; mentions Forbes/TechCrunch/Business Insider PR
- **Win factor:** Mechanism-explainer pages with branded diagrams; converts informational searchers into buyers

### 2.7 Sunset — *brand owner of the asian-flush vertical*
- Hybrid model: "The Ultimate Guide to Asian Flush and Asian Glow" + product pages
- "94% satisfied" / "75,000+ flush-free customers" social proof
- Names ingredients explicitly (NAC, DHM, L-Theanine, B-vitamins) — competes for ingredient-level queries
- **Win factor:** Single-vertical brand authority (asian flush is their sole topic)

### 2.8 Cheers Health — *Shark Tank-backed publisher*
- "Spontaneous Consumption" blog: "Dehydration isn't the main cause of hangovers," "Why you can't drink like you used to," "Best diet to love your liver"
- 700k+ reviews displayed; "40 million better mornings" social-proof metric
- Doctor endorsement video ("Dr. Do")
- **Win factor:** Counter-intuitive content angles ("dehydration isn't the cause") earn shares + backlinks

## 3. Content Gap Themes — where competitors have content DHM Guide does NOT

DHM Guide has **193 posts** but most are SEO-targeted listicles. The site is missing several high-value content **types** competitors monetize:

### 3.1 GAP — Mechanism Explainer Pages with Original Diagrams
**Competitors:** ZBiotics (/how-pre-alcohol-works, /how-your-body-breaks-down-alcohol, /how-we-engineer-probiotics)
**DHM Guide today:** has science *content* (dhm-science-explained.json, dhm-randomized-controlled-trials-2024.json) but no dedicated mechanism-illustration page with custom diagrams. ZBiotics gets backlinks because their diagram is the canonical version.
**Recommendation:** Create `/how-dhm-works` and `/how-your-liver-processes-alcohol` with original infographics.

### 3.2 GAP — Cited-Study Summary Posts ("paper-explainer" content)
**Competitors:** None do this well — but PMC papers themselves get all the citation traffic.
**Opportunity:** Be the layperson translator for the 5-7 key DHM papers (Shen 2012, Silva 2020, Tan 2023, etc.). DHM Guide currently has *one* (dhm-randomized-controlled-trials-2024.json) but should have one per landmark paper, with a "Study at a Glance" card showing N, dose, finding.
**Why it ranks:** Captures the exact-match query "Shen DHM study" / "USC DHM liver study" + earns natural backlinks from journalists/bloggers.

### 3.3 GAP — Reddit-style "I Tried It" First-Person Testing Content
**Competitors:** thequalityedit.com ("I Tested 4 Popular Options"), borealdiary.com, individual creators on TikTok/Reddit
**DHM Guide today:** Reviews are objective product analyses (no first-person testing protocol).
**Opportunity:** "I Took DHM for 30 Days After Drinking — Here's What Happened" type personal-experiment posts. Healthline and brand sites can't do this without conflict-of-interest issues.

### 3.4 GAP — Asian Flush as a Standalone Hub
**Competitors:** getsunset.com (entire site dedicated), iblush.com, andogummy.com
**DHM Guide today:** Has 3 Asian flush posts (asian-flush-vs-alcohol-allergy-comparison, complete-guide-asian-flush-comprehensive, dhm-asian-flush-science-backed-solution) but no consolidated hub that owns the SERP.
**Opportunity:** Create a top-level `/asian-flush` hub page (like /reviews and /compare exist today) that consolidates the asian flush content into a cluster Sunset competes against.

### 3.5 GAP — Authoritative "How Hangovers Work" Definitive Guide (medical-institution style)
**Competitors:** Cleveland Clinic, Healthline, my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/16627-hangover
**DHM Guide today:** Has hangover-related content (complete-hangover-science-hub, how-long-does-hangover-last) but lacks the medical-institution framing (Symptoms / Causes / Risk Factors / Complications / When to See a Doctor) that wins informational SERPs.
**Recommendation:** Restructure complete-hangover-science-hub-2025.json to use the Cleveland Clinic disease-page taxonomy + add a "medically reviewed" badge.

### 3.6 GAP — Brand/Founder Interviews and Expert Quotes
**Competitors:** Healthline (quotes Adeem Azhar, BSc Maria Jones); Flyby (David Nutt, Imperial College London quote)
**DHM Guide today:** 0 interviews/expert quotes that I could surface.
**Opportunity:** Interview USC's Jing Liang (lead author of liver-protection paper), or any pharmacologist who's published on DHM. Each gets a dedicated post with a pull-quote that other writers will cite (= backlinks).

### 3.7 GAP — Non-English / International Content
**Competitors:** Sunset markets internationally with shipping signals; nothing else does well here.
**DHM Guide has:** dhm-availability-worldwide-guide-2025.json (a start) — but no localized landing pages (UK, Canada, Australia, EU, India, Japan, Korea). Massive long-tail opportunity given Hovenia dulcis is a Korean/Japanese plant.

## 5. Top 3 Reproducible Content Patterns DHM Guide Should Adopt Site-Wide

### Pattern A — **Medical-Reviewer Byline + Last-Updated Badge**
Every Healthline, Cleveland Clinic, and WebMD page has this. It's the single biggest E-E-A-T signal that's currently missing from DHM Guide. Cost: hire one freelance MD/RD on Upwork ($150-300 per article reviewed). Implement: visible "Reviewed by [Name, Credential] | Last Updated [Date]" line at top of every post. 5-10x ranking impact for medical/health queries.

### Pattern B — **Mechanism-Explainer Pages with Original Diagrams**
ZBiotics owns "/how-pre-alcohol-works," Sunset has "How acetaldehyde processing works." DHM Guide has the *content* but not the *visualization*. Build 3-5 "How X Works" pages with custom infographics (How DHM Works, How Your Liver Processes Alcohol, How GABA Receptors Respond to Alcohol, How Acetaldehyde Causes Hangovers, How Asian Flush Genetics Work). These earn editorial backlinks naturally.

### Pattern C — **Study-Summary "Paper at a Glance" Cards**
The whole niche cites the same 5-7 papers (Shen 2012, Silva 2020, USC 2020 press, Tan 2023). Be the layperson summary. Each post: 600-1,000 words, structured as { N participants, Dose, Duration, Finding, What this means for you, Direct PubMed link }. Healthline and Cleveland Clinic don't do per-paper summaries — they bundle. DHM Guide can be the canonical translator.

**Analysis Period:** Based on Pages.csv and Queries.csv data

## Executive Summary

The top-performing page (DHM Dosage Guide) drives **57% of all site traffic** (111 of 194 clicks). Analysis reveals **dosage content dominates performance** across all metrics. The data shows clear patterns: guides outperform other content types, positions 4-7 convert 3-4x better than lower positions, and comparison pages have exceptional CTR when they rank.

### Ranking:

## 5. URL Structure Patterns

### High Performers Follow Consistent Pattern:

**Format:** `/never-hungover/{topic}-{content-type}-{year}`

**Examples:**
- `/never-hungover/dhm-dosage-guide-2025` - 111 clicks
- `/never-hungover/dhm-vs-milk-thistle-which-liver-supplement-more-effective-2025` - 9 clicks
- `/never-hungover/nac-vs-dhm-which-antioxidant-better-liver-protection-2025` - 6 clicks

**Exceptions (Tools):**
- `/dhm-dosage-calculator` - 5 clicks, 4.13% CTR
- `/compare` - 5 clicks, 2.26% CTR
- `/reviews` - 7 clicks, 1.44% CTR (hub page)

### Key Insights:

1. **Descriptive URLs convert better:**
   - "dhm-vs-milk-thistle-which-liver-supplement-more-effective-2025"
   - Longer but explicitly states comparison + value prop
   - 8.74% CTR vs 4.65% for shorter "nac-vs-dhm"

2. **Year suffix (2025) appears valuable:**
   - Most high performers have "-2025" suffix
   - Signals freshness in SERPs
   - **Pattern:** Pages with year in URL avg 5.2% CTR vs 3.1% without

3. **Root-level tools underperform content:**
   - `/dhm-dosage-calculator` only 5 clicks despite 4.13% CTR
   - `/compare` only 5 clicks despite being at position 7.1
   - **Hypothesis:** Root URLs lack SEO context vs descriptive paths

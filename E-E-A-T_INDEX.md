## KEY DECISIONS

### Decision #1: PharmD vs RD vs MD?
**Answer:** PharmD (Doctor of Pharmacy)
- Best for supplement content (dosage, safety, interactions)
- Cost-effective ($200-500 setup, $50-100 per review)
- Relevant credentials for DHM content

**See:** E-E-A-T_AUDIT_AUGMENTATIONS.md, Issue #60, Augmentation #2

### Decision #2: What citation style?
**Answer:** AMA (American Medical Association)
- Numbered superscripts (cleanest visual)
- Industry standard (Healthline, WebMD)
- Easy to implement

**See:** E-E-A-T_QUICK_REFERENCE.md, "Q: What citation style?"

### Decision #3: Show license number publicly?
**Answer:** NO
- Privacy concern
- Not standard on health websites
- Show name + credentials + specialization instead

**See:** E-E-A-T_AUDIT_AUGMENTATIONS.md, Issue #67, Augmentation #4

### Decision #4: How many articles to review initially?
**Answer:** 3-5 cornerstone articles
- Highest traffic (dosage guide, comparison posts)
- Most health claims (safety, interactions)
- Longest content (5,000+ words)

**See:** E-E-A-T_IMPLEMENTATION_ROADMAP.md, Phase 2, Week 4-5

## COMPETITOR BENCHMARKS

- Author bios with photos and LinkedIn links

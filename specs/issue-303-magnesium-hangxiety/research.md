## Mechanism summary (the science angle)

1. **NMDA antagonism (PMC8202957, PMC2645546):** Mg² ⁺ blocks the NMDA channel at rest. Alcohol exposure up-regulates NMDA expression; when alcohol clears, glutamate excess drives the hyperexcitable hangxiety state. Adequate Mg restores the block.
2. **GABA-A co-factor:** Mg supports GABA-A receptor function (which is hypoactive after alcohol).
3. **Cortisol modulation:** Mg dampens HPA-axis output — relevant for the cortisol-amplified hangxiety phenotype.
4. **Sleep architecture:** Mg deepens slow-wave sleep (PMC12412596, PMC11136869).
5. **Inflammation/oxidative stress (PMC12189353):** Mg supports antioxidant defenses depleted by acetaldehyde.

## Why glycinate is "best for hangxiety"
- Glycine itself is the second active component — it acts at NMDA glycine site AND as inhibitory neurotransmitter at the spinal cord and brainstem
- Pillar (#302) recommends glycine 3 g + Mg glycinate 200-400 mg at bedtime — glycinate gives both in one molecule
- Sleep RCT data (PMC12412596) is direct
- GI tolerance is the highest of any form

## Why citrate is "best for hangover headache"
- Citrate's mild laxative effect is desirable when constipation/sluggishness is part of the hangover
- Highest tested bioavailability per dose vs oxide (PMC6683096)
- Cheaper per gram than glycinate
- Headache mechanism: dehydration + vasodilation; Mg citrate addresses both via hydration support and vascular Mg

## Why L-threonate is "best for cognitive hangxiety"
- Magtein® raises BRAIN Mg levels (the only form with strong CNS penetration data)
- 2022 Magtein RCT (PMC9786204) showed cognitive improvements in 30 days
- Direct fit for "racing thoughts / brain fog / decision-making" component of hangxiety
- More expensive — appropriate for chronic users with persistent post-drinking cognitive issues

## Why oxide and malate are NOT recommended for hangxiety
- Oxide: bioavailability ~4% — paying for nothing absorbed
- Malate: better for muscle pain/fatigue but no specific hangxiety evidence

## Verified Primary Sources (PMC IDs confirmed via WebFetch)

### Yale 2025 Liver Mechanism Paper (THE headline cite)
- **Mehal et al., "GLP-1 Receptor Agonists Protect the Liver During Alcohol Consumption"**
- npj Metabolic Health and Disease, September 18, 2025
- Yale School of Medicine
- Source: https://medicine.yale.edu/news-article/glp-1-receptor-agonists-protect-the-liver-during-alcohol-consumption/
- Key finding: GLP-1RAs reduce Cyp2e1 enzyme → less acetaldehyde produced
- BUT: slowed alcohol metabolism → higher blood alcohol concentrations
- Direct quote (Mehal): "people using GLP-1 receptor agonists might be drinking an amount of alcohol that does not normally put them above the legal blood alcohol level, but because they are taking this drug, it does."

## Mechanism Summary (Synthesized)

How GLP-1s change drinking:

1. **Brain reward**: Bind nucleus accumbens + VTA, attenuate alcohol-induced dopamine release. Krang's pleasure dim.
2. **Satiety/craving**: GLP-1 system regulates not just food but consummatory reward broadly.
3. **Liver enzyme suppression**: Lower Cyp2e1 → less acetaldehyde → less liver damage AT THE COST of slower clearance.
4. **Gastric delay**: Slowed gastric emptying = delayed alcohol absorption — but ultimately alcohol still gets absorbed; just unpredictable timing.

## Safety Issues (synthesized)

- **Hypoglycemia** (T2D especially): alcohol + GLP-1 can drop blood sugar dangerously
- **Pancreatitis**: alcohol heavy drinking + GLP-1 = compounded risk; meta-analysis says no overall increase but case reports exist
- **GI**: nausea, vomiting, reflux substantially worsened by alcohol
- **Dehydration**: alcohol diuretic + reduced thirst on GLP-1 = bigger hangovers feel
- **Higher BAC for less alcohol** (Yale): driving/legal implications

## DHM stack considerations
- DHM works on ADH/ALDH (matters when alcohol is metabolized)
- GLP-1 reduces Cyp2e1 (a different alcohol pathway, ~10% normally but dominant in heavy drinking)
- DHM doesn't interact with GLP-1 receptor — different system
- DHM still helpful for: liver protection, GABA rebalancing, hangover relief
- DOSE: standard 300-600mg DHM unchanged on GLP-1
- Caveat: Pre-drinking ritual on GLP-1s often unnecessary because intake is naturally lower

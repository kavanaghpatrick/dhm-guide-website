## QUICK DECISIONS

### Q: Should I hire MD, PharmD, or RD?
**A: PharmD (Doctor of Pharmacy)**
- Best for supplement content (dosage, safety, interactions)
- Cost: $200-500 setup, $50-100 per review
- Credentials: "PharmD, BCPS" (Board-Certified Pharmacotherapy Specialist)

### Q: What citation style?
**A: AMA (numbered superscripts)**
- Example: "DHM reduces acetaldehyde by 70%.¹"
- Used by Healthline, WebMD, medical journals
- Clean, doesn't interrupt reading

### Q: Where do I verify PharmD credentials?
**A: State pharmacy board websites**
- California: https://www.pharmacy.ca.gov/licensees/lookup.shtml
- National: https://nabp.pharmacy/programs/nalp/
- Check: Active license, no disciplinary actions

### Q: What should author bio say?
**A: Third person, 3-6 sentences:**
- Name + credentials (real degree, not "enthusiast")
- Years of experience + specific expertise
- Why you care about topic
- Professional achievements
- LinkedIn link

### Q: Do I show reviewer's license number publicly?
**A: NO**
- Show: Name, PharmD, specialization
- Don't show: License number (privacy concern)
- Example: "Jane Doe, PharmD, BCPS - Board-Certified Pharmacotherapy Specialist"

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ "Health enthusiast" in bio (use real credentials)
❌ No medical disclaimer (liability risk)
❌ Showing license number publicly (privacy concern)
❌ Hiring reviewer without verifying credentials (fraud risk)
❌ No "Not medical advice" statement (legal requirement)
❌ Author bio in first person (should be third person)
❌ No contact information on About page (trust signal)
❌ Skipping schema markup (missed SEO opportunity)

## CITATION FORMAT (AMA Style)

**Inline:**
```
DHM reduces acetaldehyde by 70%.¹
```

**References Section:**
```
References

1. Shen Y, Lindemeyer AK, Gonzalez C, et al. Dihydromyricetin as a
   novel anti-alcohol intoxication medication. J Neurosci.
   2012;32(1):390-401. doi:10.1523/JNEUROSCI.4639-11.2012
```

**Format:**
- Authors: Last name, First Initial. (max 6 authors, then "et al.")
- Journal: Standard NLM abbreviation
- Year;Volume(Issue):Pages
- DOI if available
